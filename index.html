The Titanic impacted future sea based travel and marine activity in many more ways than one. The Titanic left such a huge mark on the laws for maritime travel. Its been over 90 years since the wreck but the effects of the wreck still exist today. After the wreck of the Titanic many laws and regulations were passed in order to prevent similar catastrophes. For example, the US Government passed the Radio Act of 1912. This act stated that all passenger ships must have radio communications established 24 hours a day with a secondary supply of power in case of emergencies.

When the Titanic hit the iceberg, there was a confusion as to what to do because of the lack of preparedness and training of the crew. None of the crew members were trained on who to contact or how to contact using their radio. Ships are now required to have trained members on the crew that are capable of using the radio in a time of need as well as fixing them incase anything goes wrong.They also created a radio channel specific for emergencies in the ocean that would run for 24 hours. This act was put in place to make sure that a ship in distress is always able to radio for help. The International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea declared the signal of a red flare shot from a ship as a sign for help.

This is put in place due to the fact that the Titanic had fired red rockets into the air but were misinterpreted by the crew members of the SS Californian who had seen the flares and believed they were to identify themselves to nearby ships. Following the sinking of the Titanic, the US Navy assigned Scout Cruisers to sweep the Grand Banks for any dangerous obstacles. The Chester and the USS Birmingham were involved in the International Ice Patrol and continued for the rest of 1912 following the Titanic.

After 1912 the Revenue Cutter Service assumed authority over the International Ice Patrol after the US Navy claimed that they couldn’t spare any of their ships. The Seneca and the Miami were sent on the expedition. The Titanic disaster led to the convening of the first International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea (SOLAS) in London, on 12 November 1913. On 30 January 1914, a treaty was signed by the conference that resulted in the formation and international funding of the International Ice Patrol. Since the mid-1900s, planes and other aircraft vehicles have become the main form for ice patrol missions. Ships with more than 50 passengers were required to have enough lifeboats to accommodate for all the passengers and crew on board. This sounds like common sense but prior to the Titanic, ships were not required to provide a spot on a lifeboat for everyone and would have to pick and choose.

There were also changes in the way ships were made in order to prevent such things from happening again. Double hulls became the most important change made to the ships being built because it strengthened the ships in order to prevent ice and other obstacles from penetrating the ship. Bulkheads were also raised to prevent the waterline from reaching over the ship. Both changes were used to make the ships stronger in order to resist the obstacles such as icebergs and other natural and artificial structures that may compromise the ships and their passengers.

We are still learning about the wreck and learning from the wreck to this day. After the Titanic’s discovery there were many people who tried to salvage as many valuable items as they could from the wreck and some that succeeded. Due to this salvagery, the US Congress passed the R.M.S. Titanic Maritime Memorial Act of 1986 which regulates the research and salvage of the shipwreck by declaring it a memorial.

If these precautions and regulations were in effect before the wreck of the Titanic, then it probably wouldn’t have happened or if it did happen, they would be more than capable of saving everyone with little effort. However, the wreck of the RMS Titanic was a wake up call for the world.The world realized that nothing is truly invincible especially if the crew onboard is not properly trained and if the ship isn’t properly equipped.
